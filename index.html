Film Score Portfolio

An aquaintence needed a way to showcase his sound design work and further his career in the film industry. I tried out Webflow and really came to appreciate how it works by using semantic HTML and CSS, despite a bit of a learning curve. You can export your designs as clean code, add/edit custom programming, and host the site wherever you choose.

I made every piece of this from scratch. The layout, the user interface components, the animations, the graphic design on the images, etc. were all made by me. I created the logo animation by splitting each piece into separate SVG files, then using a $(window).load(function(); and sequential CSS3 Transforms. The background video is some footage I edited of a vinyl record spinning. The "Down Arrow" on the landing page has a mesh speaker grill as the background, with some light CSS shadowing.

If you make your browser as narrow as possible when you arrive to the live site, you will see that I animated the mobile "hamburger" menu and placed it inside of a speaker cone that rotates in/out while the mobile menu slides into place. I take pride in my ability to conceptualize and implement custom-tailored themes and solutions to enhance the branding of any business. The rest of the site is currently unresponsive to mobile devices because I was in the middle of changing the layout breakpoints using CSS grid, but ultimately chose to place the project on hold due to unrelated issues.

About:

I'm a Developer who also understands the nuances of "design-thinking," with formal (and informal) training and proficiency in both disciplines. My priority will always be maintaining integrity, while ensuring the best user experience possible for the end product. My goal is to serve as a bridge between software engineering and design, by sympathizing with differing perspectives, and by increasing collaboration between differing roles.

I honestly never thought that I would be a computer programmer "when I grew up," due to the common stereotypes that, of course, all sounded like the total opposite of my personality. Eventually though, I got curious and discovered so much more about all of the different roles within the tech industry, all thanks the great information superhighway itself!

I’ve now come to realize that my knowledge and interest in psychology, my tendency to analyze and problem-solve every little thing, my strong sense of empathy, my perfectionism (and mild OCD) that I can pass off as part of my "work ethic," and even my artistic abilities are actually the traits that make this career perfect for me. The more that I learn and continue to expand my skillset, the more excited I become about my career as a developer.

-It could also just be the fact that I get to create COOL stuff, while genuinely enjoying what I get to do for work each day (...except for when something inevitably refuses to work... and then you realize, after HOURS of PANIC, that it was all because you forgot to put a bracket or a semicolon somewhere).
